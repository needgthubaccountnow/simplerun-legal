Vado Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 22, 2026

Vado is a running app for iPhone and Apple Watch, made by an independent developer. This policy explains, in plain language, what data the app touches, where it goes, and the choices you have.

The short version:

  • Your health and workout data lives on your device in Apple Health. It never goes to our servers unless you turn on a feature that sends specific pieces of it.
  • An account is optional. The app works fully without one. Signing in only adds cloud backup and Strava upload.
  • We do not run ads, we do not track you across other apps or websites, and we do not sell your data. Ever.
  • You can delete your account and everything on our servers from inside the app.

Who we are

Vado is built and operated by an independent developer ("we", "us"). If you have any question about your privacy or this policy, see Contact at the bottom.

What the app does with your data

Health and fitness data (Apple Health / HealthKit)

Vado records runs and reads your fitness data through Apple's HealthKit framework. Concretely:

What the app reads from Health:

  • Your running and walking workouts (so it can show your history)
  • Workout routes (the GPS track of a run)
  • Heart rate
  • Active energy (calories) burned
  • Step count
  • VO₂max estimates (the fitness number your Apple Watch calculates after outdoor runs — the app only reads it, never writes it)

What the app writes to Health:

  • The runs you record. Each run is saved as a running or walking workout, including distance, duration, heart rate, and — for outdoor runs — your route.

Where this data lives: on your device, in Apple Health. Apple, not us, controls whether your Health data syncs to your other Apple devices through your own iCloud. This data does not leave your device and reach our servers except for the specific, opt-in features described below (cloud backup and Strava). You control HealthKit access in the iOS/watchOS Settings app, and you can revoke it at any time — the app keeps working, it just falls back to what it can measure on its own.

Location and motion

  • Location (GPS): During an outdoor run, Vado uses your location to measure distance and record your route. Location is used only while you are running. On the phone, the app keeps reading location while the screen is locked mid-run (that's the only reason it uses background location) so your route and distance stay accurate.
  • Motion: When GPS is unavailable — on a treadmill or indoors — the app uses motion data to estimate your running distance.

Your route is stored with the run in Apple Health. It only leaves your device if you sign in (cloud route backup) or connect Strava — both described below.

Weather (Apple WeatherKit, on Apple Watch)

To draw the sky in the running scene, the watch app asks Apple's WeatherKit for the current conditions at your run location. This sends your approximate location to Apple, which returns the weather. That exchange is handled by Apple and covered by Apple's privacy policy; the app only keeps the few weather fields it needs to render the scene (things like cloud cover and whether it's raining). If weather can't be fetched, the app just falls back to a time-of-day sky.

Accounts and cloud backup (optional)

Vado works completely offline with no account. If you want your training data backed up and restorable on a new phone, you can create an account. You can sign in with:

  • Sign in with Apple
  • Google
  • Email and password

With Sign in with Apple you can use "Hide My Email," in which case we only ever see Apple's relay address, not your real email.

Our backend is hosted on Supabase. Every user's data is isolated by row-level security, so your account can only ever read and write its own rows.

What syncs to your account when you're signed in:

  • Run metadata — for each completed run: start time, duration, distance, average heart rate, whether it was indoor, its split times, best efforts, and a small route preview. (This is data about the run, not your raw Health records.)
  • Route tracks — the full GPS track of a run, stored as a private file in our storage bucket, reachable only by your account. This backup is also what powers Strava upload.
  • Training state — your goal and generated plan, your streak goal, and your pace/fitness profile (the VDOT model the app builds from your runs).
  • Selected settings — your display units, color theme, and whether the game tab is hidden.
  • Your display name and the anonymous identifier from your sign-in provider.

What does NOT sync to our servers: your raw Apple Health database — your full heart-rate streams, your complete workout archive, your VO₂max samples. Those stay in Apple Health on your device. We only ever store the specific run metadata, route tracks, and training state listed above.

If you never sign in, none of this leaves your device — your training data lives locally on your phone and is cleared if you delete the app.

Strava (opt-in)

Connecting Strava is entirely optional and off by default. If you connect it:

  • You authorize Vado through Strava's standard OAuth screen. We request only the permission needed to upload your runs (activity:write and basic read).
  • When you upload a run, our server builds a route file from your backed-up track and posts it to Strava as an activity. Distance, duration, and route go to Strava under your Strava account.
  • Your Strava tokens are held only on our server, encrypted, and are never shipped inside the app. The app itself never handles your long-lived Strava credentials.
  • You can disconnect Strava at any time in Settings. Deleting your Vado account also revokes Vado's access on Strava's side automatically.

What Strava does with data you send it is governed by Strava's own privacy policy.

Subscriptions and payments

Vado offers an optional paid subscription. All payments are processed by Apple through the App Store using your Apple ID. We never see or handle your card number or payment details — Apple tells the app only whether your subscription is active. Manage or cancel your subscription in the App Store / your Apple ID settings.

App integrity (App Attest)

To protect the backend from abuse and fraud, signed-in requests can carry an Apple App Attest token. This is a cryptographic check, generated by your device's Secure Enclave, that confirms a request comes from a genuine, unmodified copy of the app. It contains no personal information and is not used to identify or track you.

What we do NOT do

  • No advertising. There are no ads in Vado.
  • No cross-app / cross-site tracking. We don't use the advertising identifier (IDFA), and the app does not ask for tracking permission because it doesn't track you.
  • No third-party analytics, marketing, or crash-reporting SDKs. The app's only outside code dependencies are a local database library, the Supabase client for your own account backend, and Apple's own frameworks.
  • No selling or sharing your data. We never sell, rent, or trade your personal data, and we don't share it with data brokers.

Data retention and deletion

  • Local data: Anything stored only on your device (your local training data, and your workouts in Apple Health) is removed when you delete the app or clear it in Apple Health. Health data is controlled by you in the Health app.
  • Account data: While your account exists, we keep the synced data described above so it's there to restore. You can delete your account at any time from Settings → your account → Delete account inside the app. Deleting your account:
    • revokes Vado's access to your Strava (if connected),
    • removes your route-track files from our storage, and
    • permanently deletes your account and all of its database rows (runs, routes, settings, training state, and connection records).

This is a hard delete — it cannot be undone, and it does not touch your Apple Health data, which remains yours on your device.

Children

Vado is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal data from them.

Your choices at a glance

  • Use the app with no account — nothing syncs off your device.
  • Grant or revoke Health and Location access anytime in iOS/watchOS Settings.
  • Connect or disconnect Strava anytime in the app's Settings.
  • Delete your account and all server-side data anytime in the app.
